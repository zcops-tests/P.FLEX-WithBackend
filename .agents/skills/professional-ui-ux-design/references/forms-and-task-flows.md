# Forms and Task Flows

Use this reference when the screen centers on data entry, onboarding, settings, or multi-step tasks.

## Form design defaults

- Keep labels visible rather than relying on placeholders.
- Group related fields and separate unrelated ones clearly.
- Put the most important inputs first when possible.
- Reduce optional fields and advanced settings until they are needed.

## Validation defaults

- Validate early enough to help, but not so early that it interrupts.
- Make errors specific and next-step oriented.
- Keep error placement close to the field or action that caused it.
- Preserve data whenever correction is possible.

## Action design

- Use one dominant submit action.
- Keep secondary actions visually quieter.
- Explain destructive or irreversible outcomes before submission.

## Multi-step flows

- Split steps only when it reduces cognitive load.
- Make progress visible.
- Keep the next action obvious.
- Allow backtracking without punishment when feasible.

## Empty and success states

- Treat them as part of the flow, not afterthoughts.
- In empty states, explain what to do next.
- In success states, confirm the outcome and offer the most likely next step.
